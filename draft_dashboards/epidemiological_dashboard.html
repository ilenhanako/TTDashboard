Method — disease burden composition by SDI quintile

Raw DALY counts (thousands) are extracted from WHO GHE 2021 "All ages" sheet for three GHE top-level rows: Communicable (row 11), Non-communicable (row 73), Injuries (row 210). Counts are divided by embedded population values and multiplied by 1,000 to yield rates per 1,000 population. Countries are then merged with 2021 IHME SDI values and binned into five equal-count quintiles via pd.qcut(). The unweighted mean of each rate within each quintile is plotted.

∿OLS Regression

Three independent OLS regressions (scipy.stats.linregress) quantify how strongly SDI predicts communicable, NCD, and injury DALY rates. Residuals from the communicable model feed the anomaly detection step.

Model — Ordinary Least Squares (y = β₀ + β₁·SDI + ε)

Communicable, NCD, and injury DALY rates are each regressed independently on SDI. R² measures cross-national variance explained by development level alone. The strong communicable R² (0.613) confirms SDI as the primary driver of the epidemiological transition. The weaker NCD R² (0.285) reflects the "double burden" — NCD accumulation depends on factors beyond income convergence (urbanisation, dietary shifts, ageing). Residuals from the communicable model are saved and used as the anomaly score in the next step.

⬡K-Means Clustering

K-means applied to the standardised 4-D feature space [SDI, communicable, NCD, injury rates] identifies four coherent epidemiological transition stages. k=4 selected via elbow method.

Model — K-Means (sklearn KMeans, k=4, n_init=20)

All four features are z-score standardised with StandardScaler before clustering, so each dimension contributes equally after normalisation. k=4 was selected via the elbow method on within-cluster sum of squares across k ∈ {2,…,8}. After fitting, clusters are relabelled by ascending mean SDI (C0 = lowest, C3 = highest). Euclidean distance in z-score space implicitly treats all four dimensions equally.

⚑Anomaly Detection

Countries with communicable burden deviating more than ±1.5 SD from the OLS regression line. 14 underperformers (higher burden) and 12 overperformers (lower burden) identified.

Method — residual-based anomaly detection (±1.5 SD threshold)

Residuals εᵢ = yᵢ − ŷᵢ are computed from the communicable OLS model for each country. Countries with εᵢ > 1.5·σ carry disproportionately high communicable burden for their SDI ("underperformers"). Countries with εᵢ < −1.5·σ achieve unusually low burden ("overperformers"). σ = 77.64 DALYs/1k. The ±1.5σ band is overlaid as dashed corridors. This approach is parameter-transparent but assumes normally distributed residuals.

☀Climate Risk

WHO Heat Index 35 stratified by SDI tertile (2000–2020) and IHME SDI regional trajectories (1990–2021), revealing the compounding heat burden on low-development nations.

Analysis — heat exposure × SDI stratification

The WHO Heat Index 35 series (annual days with apparent temperature ≥ 35°C) is merged with 2021 SDI for 171 matched countries. Countries are split into three equal-count tertiles via pd.qcut(). The unweighted mean of annual heat-index days is plotted for each tertile across 2000–2020. The IHME SDI dataset provides regional aggregates (8 world regions) from 1990–2021. The overlap of high communicable disease burden, fast-rising SDI transitions, and accelerating heat exposure forms the "triple burden" framing central to the project's policy argument.
